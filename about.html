About

The Liber Consolationis Digital Archive is an ongoing research project by undergraduate students of Washington and Lee University under the direction of Professor Melissa Vise (Medieval History) and Professor Mackenzie Brooks (Digital Humanities). The project welcomes a breadth of interests including (but not limited to) medieval history and literature, classics, digital humanities, data science and coding.

Current Undergraduate Editor and Translator: Margaret Alexander, Class of 2024

Logo and initial coding by Nayongi Borthwick, Class of 2023

Where does the text come from?

While Liber Consolationis et Consilii was originally published in 1246, the Latin text used on this site is from Thor Sundby's 1873 critical print edition--the product of 15 consulted manuscripts. Sundby's edition has since been published online by Google Books. We hope that eventually we can implement scanned manuscripts into the current site.
