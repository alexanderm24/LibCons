Incipit

Liber Consolationis et Consilii (The Book of Consolation and Counsel) is a medieval Latin treatise written by the northern Italian notary Albertano da Brescia in 1246. Invoking the wisdom of classical and Christian authors, a woman named Prudentia convinces her husband Melibeus not to pursue vendetta after life-long enemies attacked both her and their daughter. Melibeus consults many individuals to determine the fate of his foes. He gathers a large demographic: the young and the old, surgeons and physicians, close friends and legal consultants—but ultimately, his wife’s words of wisdom prevail.

The tale was translated into many languages. Most notably, Geoffery Chaucer adopted it into The Canterbury Tales as “The Tale of Melibee” in 1392.

This site provides both a digitized Latin version of the story and an English translation with citations and editorial notes. Read more about the textual history and current modifications here.
